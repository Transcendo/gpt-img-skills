## Deep Design Translation

Convert theory into visible image decisions:

- **The sublime, not beauty**: prioritize awe, pressure, restraint, and cognitive overload over prettiness, charm, decoration, or warm spectacle.
- **Scale discrepancy**: show a familiar human-scale anchor next to an object that exceeds ordinary measurement; the viewer should feel that normal spatial judgment has failed.
- **Burkean obscurity**: hide enough of the object that the mind completes it; fog, crop, weather, bright mist, shadow, depth, and occlusion are core tools, not background garnish.
- **Safe terror**: keep the danger contemplative and distant; no active attack, no collapsing body, no gore, no action climax.
- **Kantian negative pleasure**: make the viewer's imagination fail to grasp the whole, then restore calm through strict composition, order, and stillness.
- **Ego shrinking**: use the witness as a measuring device, not a hero; the figure should look still, back-facing, physically vulnerable, and almost erased by scale.
- **Civilization as geology**: use monolithic geometry, pale concrete, oxidized metal, matte stone, grassland, shore, ice, ash, city-cliff repetition, and natural terrain to make engineering feel like landscape.
- **Cosmic indifference**: the structure should not care about humans; avoid facial emotion, friendly entrances, warm windows, welcoming lights, or clear purpose.

## Premium Film-Still Finish

Use this block to make the image feel expensive, cinematic, and physically photographed:

- `premium cinematic film still quality`
- `large-format cinema camera feel`
- `controlled contrast, soft highlight roll-off, preserved highlight detail`
- `open shadow detail, clean shadows with texture, no crushed shadows`
- `clear midtone separation, luminous restrained atmosphere`
- `subtle 35mm film grain, refined analog texture, clean low-noise image`
- `restrained halation around distant lights, no excessive lens flare`
- `volumetric haze creating depth and mystery while preserving legibility`
- `precise atmospheric perspective, layered depth planes`
- `muted filmic color grade, disciplined low-saturation palette`
- `naturalistic texture, no plastic CGI, no glossy concept-art render`

Avoid cheap "cinematic" shortcuts:

- overprocessed HDR
- hyper-sharp digital texture
- teal-orange cliche
- heavy bloom everywhere
- dirty vintage dust, scratches, light leaks, or damaged-film nostalgia
- muddy blacks, crushed shadows, underexposure by default, blown highlights
- glossy game-poster lighting or saturated fantasy color

## Reference-Case Composition Kernel

Use this when the subject is short, vague, or likely to collapse into a generic dark wall:

- **Tiny witness**: place one back-facing figure or sparse silhouettes in the lower 10%-25% of the frame, roughly 2%-6% of image height.
- **Single mother shape**: make one silhouette dominate: curved wall, vertical wall, hollow tunnel, canyon, city-cliff, ring segment, suspended underside, luminous city-face, or a subject-specific equivalent.
- **Landscape entry path**: use slope, shoreline, ridge, seabed, road, dune, grass line, fog line, light band, or wall edge as a visual path into the structure.
- **Breathing space**: preserve quiet grassland, water, sky, mist, dust, snow, or low-detail terrain. Do not fill the whole image with mechanical detail.
- **Edge light and haze**: prove scale with silver-white edges, pale wall faces, atmospheric separation, and distant haze; do not rely on making the scene dark.
- **Do not copy reference motifs**: learn the relationship of human, terrain, light, and giant form; do not repeat exact three curved walls, exact grass-slope placement, exact tunnel glow patches, or exact golden city-wall angle.

## Luminous Exposure And Color

Default to the high-quality reference grade: low saturation but not dark, cool but breathable, quiet but not underexposed.

Prefer:

- luminous overcast daylight
- misty high-midtone atmosphere
- open shadows with detail
- pale mineral colors
- soft silver rim light
- clear atmospheric perspective
- low-saturation daylight film look
- gray-green terrain, pale blue-gray sky, silver-white structure edges, muted ochre or restrained amber target lights

Avoid by default:

- low-key, nocturne, abyssal black, shadow-dominated, underexposed, black metal everywhere, dead blue, deep night, dark fantasy, apocalyptic gloom
- full-frame yellow filter, saturated teal-orange, neon cyberpunk unless explicitly requested

If the user explicitly asks for night, underground, abyss, or horror-adjacent subjects, keep the frame legible with open shadows, visible structure edges, and a luminous atmospheric plane.

## Cinematic Look Presets

Pick one preset silently when it helps the subject. Do not list the preset name to the user unless they ask for prompts.

- **Austere Daylight 50D**: clean daylight exterior, fine-grain film texture, cold diffuse sky, preserved highlights, crisp but not oversharpened terrain detail. Use for ice fields, salt flats, desert basins, white concrete walls, and vast daytime megastructures.
- **Luminous Overcast 250D**: bright but restrained low-saturation daylight, open shadows, silver edge light, pale blue-gray sky, high-midtone mist, clear atmospheric perspective. Use as the default for grasslands, valleys, shores, city-cliffs, curved walls, and reference-inspired images.
- **Nocturne 500T**: low-light tungsten film mood, open shadow detail, distant industrial amber lights, restrained halation, cool atmosphere, volumetric fog. Use only when the user asks for night, underground, or interior-scale darkness.
- **Large-Format Monolith**: deep focus, high dynamic range feel, steady composition, layered atmospheric depth, clean texture, monumental calm. Use for wide vistas, Bilibili-style horizontal panoramas, and high-end sci-fi stills.
- **Desaturated Coastal Bleakness**: gray-blue winter light, sea mist, wet concrete, muted coastline palette, soft backlight. Use for shore walls, flood barriers, dead ports, ocean gates, and glacial structures.
- **Sunlit Mist Relic**: restrained warm target light inside cool air, luminous dust or sea spray, pale wall faces, clear midtones, soft highlight roll-off. Use for pilgrimage, golden city-faces, sacred structures, and hopeful-but-quiet scenes.
- **Cold Industrial Cathedral**: shaped light, tall shafts of foggy light, vast interior volume, concrete/steel ribs, small amber work lights as scale scars. Use for factories, archives, data vaults, temples, and hollow mountain machines; keep shadows readable.

## Perspective And Scale

Prefer perspectives that remove mastery from the viewer:

- ground-level view from the base of the structure
- extreme low-angle shot, worm's-eye view, looking upward from terrain
- wide-angle forced perspective where the tiny witness and the structure share the same visual field
- long lens compression for a wall, gate, or city-cliff that seems to flatten the world
- horizon swallowed by the structure, with only fragments of sky left
- deep focus with atmospheric falloff for giant scale, rather than portrait-style shallow depth of field
- restrained anamorphic feeling for horizontal frames: wide composition, gentle edge falloff, subtle distant flare only when motivated by light

Avoid:

- top-down drone views unless the user explicitly asks for them
- clean aerial maps, blueprint-like explanatory views, or symmetrical product renders
- camera positions that make the viewer feel powerful, safe, or in control
- generic shallow bokeh that makes the structure feel miniature

## Obscurity And Non-Finality

Make the structure impossible to complete mentally:

- upper levels lost in dense volumetric fog
- sides cropped beyond the frame
- facade half swallowed by sandstorm, snow, sea mist, ash, or cloud
- suspended underside blocking the sky with no visible edge
- shadow gaps, service shafts, bright mist, missing perspective vanishing points
- atmospheric scattering that erases the top, end, or true geometry
- partial non-Euclidean distortion used sparingly as "scale beyond human geometry"

Do not describe every mechanism. Leave purpose, origin, and full extent unresolved.

## Monumental Order, Materials, And Light

Use restraint to create cold intelligence:

- **Form**: massive monolithic facades, curved walls, ring segments, windowless slabs, repeated ribs, grids, cut voids, archive-like shelves, dam faces, city-cliffs, state-scale vaults.
- **Material**: pale concrete, matte concrete, oxidized metal used sparingly, basalt, weathered stone, mineral scars, grass-stained surfaces, salt-stained surfaces, wind-polished walls.
- **Light**: luminous overcast daylight, pale industrial floodlight, silver edge light, distant amber slits, low sun diffused by fog, soft rim light, bright mist.
- **Lighting shape**: broad wrapped key, soft back rim light, open shadow detail, atmospheric haze, smoke or dust only when it adds depth.
- **Palette**: low saturation, concrete gray, pale blue-gray, gray-green, ash white, silver-white, muted rust, faded grass green, restrained amber. Use small warm lights as narrative targets or scale scars, not comfort.

Avoid defaulting to black metal, dead-blue night, warm golden-hour romance, glossy sci-fi glamour, neon cyberpunk excess, decorative ornament, cozy windows, or inviting entrances.

## Emotional Routes

- **Boundary**: one tiny figure stops at the last walkable ground before an impossible wall, gate, dam, cliff, city-face, quarantine edge, or world edge.
- **Pilgrimage**: sparse exhausted walkers cross a ridge or salt flat toward a distant structure, quiet rather than heroic.
- **Unreturnable Home**: a dead housing cliff, school cliff, city cliff, or faint windows like dying embers; the figure recognizes a place that can no longer be entered.
- **Cosmic Indifference**: the sky is blocked by a suspended underside, ring, orbital fragment, aurora wall, solar flare wall, planet-scale machine, god-scale form, or alien geometry.
- **Monumental Order**: a state-scale archive, temple, factory, data vault, tribunal wall, or institutional machine whose order is colder than human life.
- **Luminous Relic**: a pale curved wall, silver city-face, sea-wall, grassland ring, or misty gate that feels ancient, restrained, and unreadable without being dark.

## Scale Proof

- one tiny back-facing person
- sparse silhouettes or a thin line of walkers
- tiny vehicles, antenna towers, window slits, stairways, cable lines
- abandoned camp, broken observatory, village lights, dead ships
- flocks of birds appearing like dust against the structure
- ladders, maintenance bridges, scaffolds, or elevator slits as tiny marks
- frame crops the megastructure at top/sides; haze hides its end

## Body Anchors

- neck tilted back until it aches
- boots on cracked salt, snow crust, ash, wet sand, or black ice
- breath fading into cold mist
- wind sound swallowed by the wall or sky
- long thin shadow nearly erased by scale
- path ending before the structure begins
- shoulders hunched under the mass of the view
- figure standing still, not running, not posing

## Terrain And Atmosphere

- wind-scoured tundra, snowfield, frozen lake, polar fog
- cracked salt flat, ash plain, desert basin, tidal flat
- cliff valley, rolling grass hills, mountain shadow
- cold morning mist, dusk haze, low cloud, dying amber light
- sea horizon, pale shore, black shore when needed, glacial shelf, dead reservoir, luminous shallow sea, underwater shelf
- slow airborne particles: ash, rain, snow, dust, mist
- low-saturation palette, atmospheric perspective, restrained filmic realism

## Subject Mapping

- palace, temple, shrine, city, school: Boundary, Pilgrimage, or Monumental Order.
- hometown, apartment, childhood, memory: Unreturnable Home.
- archive, court, state, factory, school, data center: Monumental Order.
- aurora, solar flare, alien, planet, god, Cthulhu, waterdrop: Cosmic Indifference.
- beast or monster: translate the body into gates, cliffs, ribs, bridges, or canyon walls; avoid close-up portrait.
- zombie, disaster, war: show aftermath, quarantine wall, empty road, one survivor; avoid action.
- real landmark: preserve key silhouette/material cues, then enlarge into landscape-scale geology.
- soft or cute subject: convert it into a cold monumental trace, fossil, shrine, wall, or impossible habitat rather than making it adorable.
- ocean or underwater: avoid default abyssal black; use luminous water columns, blue-green haze, suspended particles, sun shafts or pale surface glow, and a giant submerged silhouette fading into depth.
- grassland or Inner Mongolia: use low horizon, open sky, wind-shaped terrain, pale wall/ring edges, tiny witness, and restrained industrial traces; avoid neon cyberpunk unless requested.

## Video Or BGM Extension

Use only if the user asks for video, motion, cover art with music mood, or a prompt for animation:

- slow continuous zoom-in, dolly-in, or almost static environmental breathing
- no quick cuts, no chase camera, no action montage
- low-BPM, slowed, reverb-heavy, deep-frequency mood language
- long reverb tail implied through cavernous space, fog, empty industrial volume, or deep sea attenuation
- tiny particles moving slowly in sync with a heavy, restrained rhythm
- reveal one partial detail from fog at the emotional peak; do not reveal the whole structure
- keep the grade consistent across frames: muted palette, preserved highlights, textured shadows, subtle grain, no sudden saturated color shifts

## Failure Fixes

- If it looks like a big building: add civilization-as-geology, landscape-scale, full object extends beyond frame, window slits and stairways only as scale scars.
- If it looks pretty instead of sublime: remove decoration, saturated color, and inviting light; add one overwhelming shape, obscurity, negative space, and human-scale defeat.
- If it lacks film quality: add premium cinema still quality, soft highlight roll-off, open shadow detail, clear midtone separation, subtle 35mm film grain, restrained halation, volumetric haze, and muted filmic color grade.
- If it looks like AI concept art: remove plastic CGI, glossy render, hyper-sharp edges, overprocessed HDR, excessive bloom, and saturated fantasy colors.
- If it looks like a cheap retro filter: remove dust, scratches, heavy light leaks, sepia nostalgia, and damaged-film artifacts; keep clean refined grain.
- If it is too flat: add edge light, atmospheric layers, foreground/midground/background separation, mist bands, terrain path, and readable shadow texture.
- If it is too dark: add luminous overcast daylight, open shadows, pale mineral colors, silver edge light, clear midtones, and explicit `not underexposed, not shadow-dominated`.
- If it lacks emotion: make the tiny witness still, back-facing, exhausted, physically vulnerable, swallowed by scale.
- If it becomes heroic: add documentary distance, no hero pose, no triumph, no dramatic character lighting.
- If it becomes horror action: remove attack, gore, chase, battle, and close-up creature features; restore contemplative safe terror.
- If it is too clear: crop the top/sides, hide the end in fog/shadow/weather, and reduce total legibility.
- If perspective feels wrong: move camera to the ground/base and make the viewer look upward or across a vast flat terrain.
- If nature is weak: add wind-carved terrain, snow basin, salt flat, cliff shadows, natural forces shaping the scene.
- If it is too busy: use empty terrain, one quiet focal human, simple horizon, sparse silhouettes only.
- If text appears: strengthen no text, no logo, no watermark, no signature, no readable signs, no typography.
